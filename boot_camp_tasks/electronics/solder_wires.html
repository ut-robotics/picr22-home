Solder wires with 2mm bullet connector to PCB

  1. Cut about 5 cm pieces of red and black wire.

  2. Strip about 5 mm of insulation from each end of the wire.

  3. Lightly twist the strands of the wire at each end to prevent the strands from separating.

  4. Add some flux to the ends.

  5. Cover the ends with some solder. Make sure to evenly coat all around the wire.

  6. Add solder into the ends of the bullet connectors.

  7. Remelt the solder in the connector and at the end of the wire and push the wire into the connector.

  8. Keep the wire straight until the solder solidifies.

  9. Add some solder to the pads on the PCB.

  10. Place the end of the wire onto the pads and remelt the solder on the pad and at the end of the wire.

  11. Keep the wire in place until the solder has solidified.

  12. Cut pieces of heatshrink. Red for red wire and black for black wire. Heatshrink should cover all the conductive parts when the connector is connected and about 5 mm of the wire insulation.

  13. Place the heatshrink onto the connector and heat it with hot air. Suitable temperature is around 150 - 200 degrees.
